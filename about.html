I was born in Myanmar (Burma), moved across countries as a refugee, and eventually grew up in Portland, Oregon, experiences that shaped my resilience, adaptability, and deep sense of community. Currently, I’m a Computer Science student passionate about AI, data science, and building tools that make a difference. I’ve worked as an AI & Data Science intern developing analytics tools for social good, and as a Junior Software Engineer creating a public transportation safety app. My projects often bridge technology and social impact from building an EEG-based brain-computer interface for accessibility, to producing short films, to supporting refugee and ethnic minority communities. At the heart of my work is a commitment to using technology and storytelling to empower communities and improve lives.

AI & Data Science – Charity Navigator

Built scalable analytics pipelines and machine learning models to guide nonprofit decision-making.

Designed a charitable giving index and an LLM architecture to predict donor and nonprofit engagement trends.

Software Engineering – URBAN TXT: Teens Exploring Technology

Developed a public transportation safety app used by over 2,000 residents in Los Angeles, integrating real-time crime reports and partnering with city transit and cybersecurity teams.

Enhanced user safety, retention, and engagement through data-driven features and community collaboration.

AI Research & Creative Technology – SHTEM at Stanford University

Researched the ethical and creative dimensions of AI, publishing findings and building AI-powered interactive games.

Used generative AI tools like DALL-E and Midjourney to explore technology’s artistic and educational potential.
