    • I've been crafting short films since I was 14, though my passion for the art of filmmaking sparked when I was 8. In high school, I founded and led our Film Club, growing it to 20+ members. We'd dissect films, dive deep into the craft, and then go shoot our projects. I wrote and directed my first 3 short horror and mystery films through those sessions.

    I'm currently working with Stanford's Flying Horse Films, a student-run production company where I take on roles as director of photography and assistant camera. I love working directly with the camera; it's where storytelling comes alive.

    For my own films, thrillers, horror, and mystery are my thing because I love keeping audiences guessing. These days, though, I'm pushing myself into different genres to see what other stories I can tell.
