About Me

I was born in Myanmar (Burma), moved across countries as a refugee, and eventually grew up in Portland, Oregon, experiences that shaped my resilience, adaptability, and deep sense of community. Currently, I'm a Computer Science student passionate about AI, data science, and building tools that make a difference. I've worked as an AI & Data Science intern developing analytics tools for social good, and as a Junior Software Engineer creating a public transportation safety app. My projects often bridge technology and social impact—from building an EEG-based brain-computer interface for accessibility, to producing short films, to supporting refugee and ethnic minority communities. At the heart of my work is a commitment to using technology and storytelling to empower communities and improve lives.

Hunger Map Optimizer

Built for the Stanford CS109 Probability Challenge. A probabilistic model simulating a volunteer driving around a city to rescue leftover meals from restaurants before they spoil. Combines uncertain food amounts, spoilage times, and routing decisions into a single random variable to compare strategies (Greedy, Nearest, Random) and estimate which performs best on average. Addresses the intersection of food waste and food insecurity through probability modeling.

Lights, Camera, Action!

I've been crafting short films since I was 14, though my passion for the art of filmmaking sparked when I was 8. In high school, I founded and led our Film Club, growing it to 20+ members. We'd dissect films, dive deep into the craft, and then go shoot our projects. I wrote and directed my first 3 short horror and mystery films through those sessions.

I'm currently working with Stanford's Flying Horse Films, a student-run production company where I take on roles as director of photography and assistant camera. I love working directly with the camera; it's where storytelling comes alive.

For my own films, thrillers, horror, and mystery are my thing because I love keeping audiences guessing. These days, though, I'm pushing myself into different genres to see what other stories I can tell.
